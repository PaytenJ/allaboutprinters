    How a Laser Printer Works

    Though it is unknown how the original printers worked
    the laser printer can be fully described here on how it operates. They are actually extremely similar to the photocopiers
    with a minor differents. Ink particles transfer from a laser which is guided by the computer, it then 'fuses' to the
    paper. This is done by a roler inside the printer to make sure the ink is completley transfered onto the paper.

    Chester Carlson was the first inventor of the computer printer. The name he had called his printer was Xerox,
    which was invented in 1938. Chester was an American citizen born in February 8, 1906 in Seattle, Washington, United States
    and died on September 19, 1968 in New York City, New York, United States of America. The reason he had created this amazing invention
    was because while in collague he needed to take notes in one of the law textbooks he could not afford and wanted to find a way to make
    duplicates of the same document.
